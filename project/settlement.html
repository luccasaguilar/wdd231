Be prepared

Here you'll find useful tips for your preparation—from language learning and what to do before coming to Canada to arriving at the airport and key points to consider during your first few weeks in Canada.

Learn English and French

Knowing both official languages can significantly improve your integration, job opportunities, and daily life in Canada. Here are 5 practical tips to start learning now:

  • Join online language courses such as Duolingo, Coursera, or Babbel to build vocabulary and grammar skills.
  • Watch movies and TV shows in English and French with subtitles to improve listening and comprehension.
  • Practice speaking with language exchange partners through apps like Tandem or HelloTalk.
  • Read books, newspapers, or blogs in your target language daily to expand your understanding of everyday vocabulary.
  • Label household items in English and French to help you memorize useful words and phrases naturally.

How to Prepare Before Arrival

  • Make sure you meet ALL the requirements before starting your application for any of the immigration pathways.
  • Gather and securely store all essential immigration documents (passport, COPR, visas).
  • Research the province or city you plan to live in to understand climate and lifestyle.
  • Open an international bank account or prepare funds for initial expenses.
  • Find temporary accommodation for your first weeks in Canada.
  • Enroll in pre-arrival settlement programs to learn about Canadian culture and job market.
  • Collect academic and professional certifications for credential recognition.
  • Make copies of medical records and vaccination history.
  • Plan for health insurance coverage during your first days.
  • Familiarize yourself with Canadian laws and rights for newcomers.

Your Arrival

  • Present your immigration documents to a border officer and answer questions truthfully.
  • Receive your Confirmation of Permanent Residence (COPR) and review details carefully.
  • Apply for your Social Insurance Number (SIN) at Service Canada.
  • Get your health card from the provincial health service office.
  • Exchange currency or open a Canadian bank account for daily use.
  • Arrange local mobile phone service and internet connection.
  • Find temporary or permanent housing that fits your needs and budget.
  • Register for newcomer orientation sessions offered by local settlement agencies.
  • Get a transit card or learn about public transportation in your city.
  • Begin networking with other newcomers and local communities.

First Weeks in Canada

  • Attend free English or French language classes to improve communication skills.
  • Visit employment resource centers to update your resume and search for jobs.
  • Register children in school and learn about local education systems.
  • Understand your rights as a tenant and sign rental agreements carefully.
  • Open a credit card to build a Canadian credit history.
  • Get a family doctor or register with a walk-in clinic for health services.
  • Learn how to navigate Canadian grocery stores and affordable shopping options.
  • Explore community centers to meet people and integrate socially.
  • Schedule an appointment for a Canadian driver’s license if you plan to drive.
  • Start building savings and understanding the Canadian tax system.
